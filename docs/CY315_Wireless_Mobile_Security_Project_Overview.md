 Project Overview Guidelines
 Research-Based Project and Optimization Tracks
1. Project Overview
Modern wireless networks (5G/6G, IoT, Wi-Fi 6) face sophisticated threats at both the protocol and
physical layers. Standard security often fails against intelligent jamming, cross-layer attacks, or
resource-constrained environments.
Students are required to conduct a research-based project that either critically evaluates existing WMS
solutions (Survey Track) or optimizes/enhances a specific security mechanism (Implementation Track).
The goal is to move beyond basic concepts to address:
 Robustness: Protecting communication against active eavesdropping or interference.
 
 Efficiency: Optimizing security overhead for mobile/constrained devices.
 
 Contemporary Solutions: Applying Physical Layer Security (PLS) or advanced cryptography to modern
 wireless problems.
2. Project Tracks
Track A: Implementation & Optimization Track
For students choosing the implementation track, the goal is to implement, evaluate, or optimize a specific
wireless security mechanism. Projects can focus on physical layer security, jammer mitigation, key
exchange protocols in mobile networks, or lightweight authentication systems. Deliverables must include
fully documented simulation or implementation code (e.g., in Python, NS-3, or MATLAB) and a
comparative performance report.
Track B: Research Survey & Literature Review Track
For students choosing the survey track, the goal is to perform a rigorous, systematic literature review on a
contemporary wireless security paradigm. Your review must identify at least 15-20 high-quality recent
papers, categorize them into thematic taxonomies, critically identify research gaps, and propose solid
future directions. The resulting paper must follow the IEEE conference formatting guidelines.
